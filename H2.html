What Cause It

Causes

So far we have defined the problem of anemia according to its effects and impact, but what causes anemia? This question will help us to complete our problem and focus our solution.

We have identified two main causes, which are Inadequate intake of iron and micronutrients, and Increased morbidity (diarrhea, parasitosis, malaria, among others). Of these, other secondary causes branch out which we will develop below.

Increased morbidity:

Access to health services:
As a result of the evolution of the different segments of the system, during 2011 the population covered by some health insurance reached 64.2% of the total, exhibiting an increasing trend as of 2004.
Coverage in rural areas exceeds coverage in urban areas; in the former it reaches 78.4% of the population, in 2011, while in the latter, it reaches 61.8%. However, within the urban area there is a different behavior between Metropolitan Lima and the rest of urban areas[27]

Lack of sanitation:
The data indicate that about 87.0% of the inhabitants have access to basic sanitation services. However, this indicator also shows that there is significant variability at the regional level. In 6 of the 25 regions, the sanitation indicator reaches values above 90.0%, with Callao being the region with the highest relative value (96.8%), while the lowest values are observed in Ucayali, Pasco and Loreto, with rates of access to sanitation services of 39.7%; 55.4% and 55.9%, respectively.[28]

Inadequate intake of iron and micronutrients:

Citizen participation:
Up to now, one of the main strategies proposed by the state to reduce anemia was to carry out campaigns, with which it was hoped to reduce the prevalence to 19% by 2021; however, these did not exceed 15 thousand sieves which demonstrated the limited scope of this method as it was approached. However, this changed when a mega campaign was held on October 29, 2017, which was presented as a "cultural link", in which all of MINSA's resources were mobilized: more than 600 health care posts were built at the national level, located in the square, parks, markets, among other places of recurrence; health personnel came out with megaphones to summon people; Stalls were installed, not only for screening and delivery of medicines, but also for nutritional tasting and counseling, and even for festive activities. Telemanagement was used for the first time, connecting at the same time to the 25 regional health directorates and managements, but each one, with its particular strategy of meeting with its population, whether it is brigadistas, health establishments open on holidays or health care tents. All this led to an unprecedented achievement, more than 154,000 children and pregnant women screened at the national level.[26]

Low family income:
Iron deficiency can be prevented by dietary modifications, fortification of food and supplementation with nutritional iron. The ideal way to prevent iron deficiency is through an adequate diet, which is not always possible to achieve due to economic limitations or deep-rooted habits.[23]
Similarly, related studies affirm the existence of a direct relationship between the socioeconomic stratum and the prevalence of iron deficiency[24], for this reason we conclude that populations with fewer economic resources are considered to be at risk for the presence of iron deficiency.[25]

Food availability:
Food availability can be defined as safe and permanent access to a sufficient and adequate level of nutritious and safe food that meets the food needs of all family members throughout the year. There is a direct correlation between a sufficient and varied diet and the state of nutrition and health of the family members. Different experiences show that when the person does not consume the necessary quantity and quality of food, it is difficult to fight the diseases even when there are efficient health services and a healthy environment.

Nutriotional disinformation:
An inadequate and unbalanced diet is not always the result of a shortage of food or lack of money to buy it; It can also be related to the ignorance of the family about the principles of good nutrition and nutrition. The major causes of death, illness and disability in which diet and nutrition play an important role include coronary heart disease, stroke, hypertension, atherosclerosis, obesity, some forms of cancer, Type 2 diabetes, osteoporosis, dental caries, gall bladder disease, dementia and nutritional anemias (iron deficency and megaloblastic). Iron deficiency has been pointed out as the most frequent cause of anemia in the world. The ignorance of the value and proper ways of eating some foods, as well as the inherited taboos, cause that some families do not have a consistent and rational attitude towards various foods of great nutritional value and thus hinder the development of eating habits that promote optimal and healthy nutrition. The main results of a study conducted in our country showed that peruvians manifest high levels of ignorance and lack of information on nutrition issues. In addition, it was observed that, in view of this generalized ignorance, peruvians show great interest in receiving nutritional information.


With this information we have identified nutritional disinformation as the main cause of our problem since, informing anemia is the most effective form of prevention.

What Has Been Done

State of Art

Once we have indentify nutrional disinformation as the main cause, we must investigate what has been done about it.
For the state of art, we selected three national or local public politics under validity and effectiveness filters. Then, we selected an international strategy to be our framework in order to elaborate a comparative table to analyze the scopes of our policies.

The first strategy that would be analyze is the National Strategy for the Reduction of Maternal and Child Anemia. 2016 to 2021 [3], which aims< to reduce nutritional anemia ((i.e) Anemia caused by inadequate nutritional habits) in pregnant women, children under 2 years and women between menarche and menopause. This strategy has five fundamental areas of intervention, which are Health and Education services, Water and Sanitation, Education for the mother or the attorney, Participation and Monitoring. This areas are addressed in the following lines of action:

Capacity strengthening Strengthening of basic services Indicence - advocacy Public Comunication Innovation Monitoring - Evaluation
Standardization workshops: Improvement of the prenatal and neonatal care and improvement of the integral care for the child Health services: Availability of medicines and equipment, Mandatory hemoglobin screening, Implementation fo protocols for adolescent micronutrient supplementation and Surveillance of the food fortification Joint work with regional governments, thought the programming of resources (type and amount) and the monitoring of the Integrated Packages (Integral Health consults) Implementation of a radio program to promote dialogue Have contest funds for innovative projects aimed at reducing malnutrition and anemia Control panel: Control of information -assuring the quality of data- sent by each designated sector and Dissemination and use of the Regional Board MIREGION and District Board MIDISTRITO
Workshops for operators: Education about anemia and its consequences. Practical sessions of alimentation Water and sanitation services: Improvement of sanitation, Water diagnosis (quality), Improve of the reservoirs and availability of water and Continuos repair and maintenance of water sources Articulation of actors: Strengthening of the multi-stakeholder platform and Improve relations between stakeholders Implementation of radio novels to promote dialogue Works for taxes: Sanitation in places with high concentration of affected children and Establishment of specialized laboratories Reports generation: Implementation of breakout discussion tables and Determination of critical points toward the anemia prevalence
Continuos training through virtual courses Agriculture and education: Promote the availability of iron-rich food (ARH), Food Fortification and biofortification, Incorporation of healthy practices and the importance of anemia prevention into the student curriculum and Strengthening of social skills to prevent child pregnancy Participation of civil society: Follow-up meetings and training oriented to the family and the community Mass campaigns: Reinforce the information that is handled about anemia and its effects on the development of children, as well as the necessary interventions to prevent it Participation of the private community: Promote corporate social responsibility in issues related to malnutrition and anemia
Joint work with local governments: Use of the Nominal Register and Implementation of local strategies in orden to reach families Social movilization: Promote the participation of mothers and fathers in the local activities
Events and campaign launching: Transmit information through campaigns and events Online comunication: Build a community that knows and shares experiences about anemia reduction
Breakfasts and visits: Personalized visits to journalists Establish a press office

The second strategy that would be analyze would be the Action Plan: Control of Anemia in the Arequipa Region [4]. It was designed and organized aiming to change the attitude of the professionals and technicians who are involved directly in reducing the anemia and malnutrition prevalence in Arequipa. In this context, they implement the following plan of action:

Strengthening of the interventions Strengthening of capacities Communicative action in health Health infrastructure Technological-scientific research and investment projects
Implementation of a monitoring program focused in searching for children under 3 years old who do not attend frequently to their dates in health facilities Implementation of a course about the ethics of communication, with the anemia issue approach Implementation of planificación workshops in which, after the presentation of a methodology, health profesional are gathered in groups to analyze the anemia prevalence Estimate the gap between the equipment of diagnosis and the required supplies, as well as the adequate infrastructure of the health facilities and the percentage of the budget needed to close the gap Formation of profesional teams that will generate operative proposals to formulate protocols of investigation and manage their execution so that their results contribute to the enhancing of the actual interventions
Differentiation of the demonstrative nutrition sessions, according to children ages in order to prevent children rejection attitudes toward food Implementation of a course named Interculturality in health aimed to the correct interpretation of the anemia prevalence from the point of view of the children and pregnant women Selection of 10 to 12 parents for each Micro Health Network at least twice a year to develop a session to explore their expectations and perceptions toward the anemia issue Evaluation of the nutritional situation of the families and estimation how many of them need support with kitchens
Elaborate a street sketch to map every house where a child under 3 years old with anemia lives. Information about time of diagnosis, characteristics of the child, adherence to the ferrous sulfate treatment and assistance to the demonstrative sessions are also included Implementation of a visit from a group of health facilities to another, approximately once a year, in order to exchange experiences about the control of anemia Elaboration and distribution of educative material about anemia, by creating posters, banners and others
Monthly generation of reports about the development of activities, analyzing constraints and the way they were solved Implementation of an online communication network to not only exchange experiences, but also share news and articles of interest about the issue Selection of responsables for the use of massive communication media at a local level
Development of an academic event (seminar) with actualized information about anemia and chronic desnutrición Implementation of workshops to strength the instrumental capacities in order to reduce the error range when diagnosing anemia by measuring hemoglobin concentrations Formation of spoke-persons, especially mothers that play the role of propagandists
Implementation of a system of coordination and selection for the most capable professionals to apply the methods and techniques for measuring hemoglobin, and to the people who is measured, in order for them to prepare reports about the effectiveness of the results

The third strategy that would be analyze is the Local Action Plan for the Infancy and the Adolescence of Antauta [5,6], which was implemented in Antauta, Puno, by the NGO Sambi and a mining company MINSUR and has reduce the prevalence of anemia from 98,8% to 76% between 2016 and 2017. According to the project manager, the role that the NGO played was to train, strengthen and facilitate the articulation between the local authorities and the population. To active they goal, they proceed with the following line of action:

  • Implementation of the vitamin supplement Feranin (polymaltose iron)

  • Periodic controls of hemoglobin concentration in children under 3 years old

  • Implementation of demonstrative sessions about adequate food habits

  • Implementation of a playroom in the second floor on the Antauta market in order to dispose of a place to capacitate about primary child care

  • Creation of the community agents, who are voluntary mothers who capacitate children in alimentation and cognitive development

  • Daily following of the families adherence to the Feranin treatment, as a labor of the community agents

  • Implementation of greenhouses know as Productive Chacras, which aim to contribute to improve the local diet and the food security

The last plan that will be analyze are the Strategies to prevent Anaemia: Recommendations from an Expert Group Consultant [7], which were elaborated to provide policy, technical and operational recommendations on strategies to reduce anemia, and to identify research need in the South-East Asia region. In this context, is worth noting that this plan was specifically selected because of its validity and the similarities in the anemia prevalence with the peruvian communities, in terms of sanitation, population, access to health care and, the anemia index.Synthesizing, the lines of action propose by the South-East Asia Regional Office are the following frameworks about prevention.
